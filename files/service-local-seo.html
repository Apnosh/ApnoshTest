Everything you want to know about local SEO

Local SEO is the process of optimizing your online presence so your business appears in local search results — especially the Google Maps 3-pack. It matters because 46% of all Google searches have local intent, and the top 3 map results get the vast majority of clicks. If you’re not there, your competitors are getting those customers instead.
Most clients see measurable improvements in 30–60 days, with significant ranking gains within 90 days. Citation building typically impacts rankings in 30–60 days. Local SEO compounds over time — the longer you invest, the stronger and more durable your rankings become.
Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is your free business listing on Google. It controls how you appear in Google Maps and local search results. It is the single most important local SEO asset for any business that serves customers in a geographic area. If you don’t have one or it’s not optimized, you’re missing out on significant local traffic.
Citations are mentions of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) on other websites — directories like Yelp, Yellow Pages, Apple Maps, and dozens more. Google uses citation consistency as a trust signal. If your NAP is different across sites, Google loses confidence in your data and your rankings suffer. We build and maintain consistent citations across 50+ directories.
