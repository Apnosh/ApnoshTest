Our story

We built the agency
we always wished existed.

Apnosh was started by marketers who spent years watching small businesses get overcharged, under-served, and locked into contracts that benefited everyone except them. We decided to build something better.

Mission: Make agency-quality marketing accessible to every business owner — not just the ones who can afford a $5,000/mo retainer.
How we got here

The problem we couldn’t
stop thinking about

Between our team, we’d worked inside agencies, in-house at growing companies, and as independent consultants. The same pattern kept showing up: the businesses that needed great marketing the most — independent restaurants, service contractors, local retailers, wellness studios — were the ones most consistently failed by the agencies they hired.

Not because agencies were malicious. But because the traditional agency model is structurally misaligned with small business needs. High overhead requires high retainers. High retainers require bundled packages. Bundled packages mean you pay for services you don’t need. And lock-in contracts mean you’re stuck regardless of results.

“We kept asking: what if we started from first principles? What does a marketing agency actually need to look like to serve a business owner fairly?”

The answer was Apnosh. Built on a modular, transparent, AI-augmented model that lets us deliver the same quality work at dramatically lower cost — and pass every cent of that savings directly to the people we serve.

What we stand for

Three commitments we make
to every client

📖
Radical transparency

Every price is published. Every deliverable is documented. Every result is reported in plain language. You will never receive an invoice that surprises you, and you will never wonder what we’re doing or why.

We don’t report impressions and reach. We report leads generated, rankings moved, revenue attributed, and calls received. If the number doesn’t connect to revenue, we don’t put it in the report.

How we operate

The principles that guide
every decision we make

01
Honesty before comfort

If a service isn’t the right fit for your business right now, we’ll tell you — even if it means a smaller invoice. Short-term honesty builds long-term relationships. We’d rather lose a sale than lose your trust.

In practice: If a client asks about a service that won’t move the needle for their stage, we recommend what will — and explain why.
02
Simplicity as a feature

Marketing doesn’t need to be complicated — it needs to be consistent. We remove jargon from every report, every proposal, and every conversation. If a business owner can’t immediately understand what we’re doing and why, we’re doing it wrong.

In practice: Every report we send is written for a business owner, not a marketer. Plain language, real numbers, clear next steps.
03
Speed as respect

Business owners don’t have time for 8-week onboarding processes. Being fast is how we show we respect the urgency of running a business. From plan to live in 7 days is a commitment, not a goal.

In practice: Day 1 plan, day 2 onboarding call, days 3–4 setup, day 7 first deliverables. Every time, for every client.
04
Ownership stays with you

Every asset we create — your website, your email list, your brand files, your Google Business Profile — belongs to you. If you ever leave, you take everything with you. No hostage-taking, no lock-in, no proprietary platforms that trap your data.

In practice: We hand over all logins, all files, and full documentation on day one — not after you cancel.

Our philosophy

Marketing shouldn’t be a black box you just pay into.

Too many business owners have had the same experience: hire an agency, pay a retainer, receive a monthly report full of graphs they don’t understand, and eventually cancel because nothing tangible changed. The agency walks away with the money. The business owner walks away more skeptical of marketing than when they started.

We think this happens for one reason: misaligned incentives. The traditional agency model rewards agencies for retaining clients — not for delivering results. Big retainers, complex reports, and confusing jargon all serve the same purpose: making it hard for the client to evaluate whether they’re actually getting value.

“If a business owner can’t look at our work and immediately understand whether it’s working, we haven’t done our job.”

Apnosh was built on a different belief: marketing should be legible. You should be able to look at your Google ranking, your review count, your website conversion rate, and your email revenue — and know, without a call with your account manager, whether the investment is working.

That’s why we publish every price, report every real metric, and keep everything month-to-month. Not because it’s better for business — in some ways, lock-in contracts and opaque reporting would be easier for us. But because it’s the right way to treat someone who is trusting us with their marketing budget and their business growth.
